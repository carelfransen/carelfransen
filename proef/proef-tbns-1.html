VIA EUROPA

Thorsten Baensch

The last couple of weeks I have worked on the first sketches for your new book project. As I told you in a previous email, while I still think VON ORT ZU ORT works really well as a book, I feel that this book needs its own design approach to set it apart. It's not just a second part in a series. It's a project of itself. Plus, while VON ORT ZU ORT had very consistent materials to work with for each stage, this new book is much more varied in its content.

Concept

After some deliberations and sketching, I came up with a much more raw approach to the design. Almost "punk" if you want. And I think this pairs well with the nature of the project and imagery. Note that all the sketched spreads I'm showing here are still rough around the edges. But they do give a good idea of the general design approach and feeling I'm aiming for.

Basically I want to use a monospaced serif typewriter font, paired with a very neutral sans serif for the titles and headings. I also opted to create a margin for all the text pages filled with an almost ASCII-art like grid pattern. I wanted to make a reference to scrapbooking in a way, also with how I treat the positioning of images (either strictly inline or loosely placed, and even slightly rotated sometimes). I am still experimenting with this a lot, so especially images placements are still very rough here. One thing I have tried to do, is to use most or all images at true size. I don't know for sure yet if this is necessary or even desirable. But with the current size of the book and the sizes of a lot of the materials, it could work very well.

I also want to translate this raw feeling to the maps in the book. There would be one map for each trip, in a similar place as in VON ORT ZU ORT. But now they are more raw in nature, using mostly black lines and shapes. I also opted to not draw an actual line for the route, but instead only highlight the main stops along the route (and maybe the other stops as well). This automatically creates a clear path. Also here, I'm still experimenting with the exact style of these maps, and I'm still considering adding/changing various elements. But this gives you a good idea of the general look and feel.

For the images in VON ORT ZU ORT, we opted to replace two printing inks with similar spot colours. I have not applied the same trick here yet, but it's something I'm definitely considering, even if only as a reference to the previous book. I also really liked how it gave all the images a very consistent and retro feel. Although I must say that you have a much larger number of analogue photographs this time around, so the need for such an approach is less.

Structure

For the structure of the book, I envision each trip to have its own section of a certain number of pages. This can vary a lot. I can definitely see it work that some trips only have 8 pages, others might have 16, 20 or 24 pages, for example. All depending on the length of the trip and the amount of available materials for each. The size of the book is currently quite similar to VON ORT ZU ORT at 230 × 320 mm.

I also played with the idea to have different kind of sections inbetween every now and then, just like the THEMEN in VON ORT ZU ORT. I have now sketched these with a full bleed background colour, for which I can also imagine using coloured paper. I can also see it work to use multiple different colours throughout the book, to emphasise this eclectic feel. Note that I have quickly threw some busstop images together on these pages. This I'm not really happy with yet, so it's something I definitely want to experiment with more.

Title

For the title, I feel that FROM PLACE TO PLACE might not be the most ideal pick. Partly because I feel it shouldn't be a direct reference to VON ORT ZU ORT, but also because I feel it doesn't really capture the essence of the project. So I brainstormed a bit and came up with VIA EUROPA. I think this title works on multiple levels. It references the idea of travelling through Europe, but also the idea of "via" as in "by way of" and the Roman word for "road", emphasising the indirect nature of these journeys. It also has a nice ring to it, I think.

I also added a subtitle for now. I have considered a number of different options, but in the end I now went for GRAND TOUR 37. Very short and simple, and referencing the two aspects that are key to the project: the idea of a "grand tour" through Europe, and the 37 journeys that were made.

Cover

For this book I wanted to give a small nod to the approach used in VON ORT ZU ORT, but still make it very much its own thing. So while still using bold, black typography, I now envision the book to be a hardcover instead, bound with a light grey linen cloth. In this current design sketch I also played around with the same ASCII-like grid patterns that I used in the book itself, and added a bold spine design. I'd very much like a quote similar to the one used on the back cover of VON ORT ZU ORT, maybe you have some ideas for that?

Next steps

Needless to say, this is still going to be a lot of work to design. But we'll take it step-by-step, beginning with finalising the overall design approach and structure. But keep in mind, the sketches are still a bit rough, so mostly focus on the overall feeling. I'm looking forward to hearing your thoughts on that!
